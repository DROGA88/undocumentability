undocumentability
undocumentability & smuggling through loophole cinema
This site functions as a public facing archive of my moving image research, investigating speculative forms of undocumentability and smuggling through what I call loophole cinema. Emerging from my experiences growing up as an undocumented immigrant in the United States, my research began with the question: how does an undocumented documentary filmmaker document themself? Through this inquiry, I explore the ontological loopholes between bureaucratic and cinematic documents and how these forms might contaminate each other. The media I present here is entangled with existential decisions such as self-deportation and exile, treating moving images as a medium of smuggling myself through borders and exile as a methodology of transmission. This is my autonomous platform that is stateless and enacts a sort of defection from transnational platform capitalism. It is here where I track my experiments, equations and formulations related to my research.
